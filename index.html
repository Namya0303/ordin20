About Us

It all started 16 years ago. A fusion of creativity, talent and skill is what Ordin@trix is all about. Ordin@trix has evolved through the years moving from an interschool festival at the state level to an international event. Over the years we have had students from China, Muscat, Japan and other countries as regular participants.

The event has witnessed gracious presence of celebrities like Mr. Amol Gupte and Miss Manju Singh apart from experts from various technical fields. The movies showcased here have been screened at the movie festivals by PVR Cinemas and CHINH India Festival.

Since it's conception, Ordin@trix has come a long way and so have the PYROTECHS. They are the very nuclei of Ordin@trix, who create fireworks in the field of technology. They breathe life into Ordin@trix.

PYROTECH members are an elite team of student computer enthusiasts. They have represented the school in various competitions and won laurels in events such as web designing, software development, image editing, movie making, 3-D modelling, Robotics, photography and others.

GUIDELINES

  1. ONLINE registrations on a first come first served basis will be entertained.
  2. The first 20 registrations will be accommodated in each event. Registrations will close on 15 July 2020.
    You can register here.
  3. List of registered schools will be displayed on the event website and on our Facebook page.
  4. The timings and links to attend the final round of events ( wherever mentioned) will be shared soon after the registrations.
  5. Submissions must not: violate any third party rights, including, but not limited to, copyrights, trademark rights, or rights of privacy and publicity; contain defamatory statements; include threats to any person, place, business, or group; or be obscene or indecent.
  6. Please refer to our school website, and/or our event website for the latest updates on the events.
  7. Decisions of the organizers & jury members regarding any issue related to Ordin@trix’20.0 will be FINAL AND BINDING on all schools and participants.
  8. To qualify as an OVERALL WINNER , a school must take part in a minimum of 4 events.
  9. Only one team per event per school should register except for Crypt@trix which has individual registrations.
  10. The students ‘ certificates and Honorary Certificate for the Winning School will be shared in electronic form a week after the event.

Innovate@trix

Vulnerability is the birthplace of innovation creation and change.

  1. The event is open for students of classes IX-XII.
  2. Only 1 team consisting of 3 students can participate from each school, with one CEO, CFO and CTO each.
  3. Each start-up must fall under the scope of one of the categories listed below. If a start-up fails to do so, the project will receive a less accurate evaluation and may even be penalised.
    1. Social Start-Ups
    2. FMCG Start-Ups
    3. Financial Start-Ups
    4. Tech Start-Ups
    5. Surprise Us
  4. Referencing online materials – for understanding the topic, providing proof for a statement or any other purpose – is allowed; however, plagiarism will not be tolerated;and will lead to disqualification.

    5. Qualification Round

  5. The qualification round will begin from 16 July and will end on 18 July. Top 10 teams will go in the final round.
  6. The list of qualifiers would be uploaded on the event website on 20 July after 12pm.
  7. Participants have to create a powerpoint presentation explaining their startup idea.
  8. The powerpoint must have at max. 10 slides covering the following points:
    1. Executive Summary
    2. Target Market
    3. Competitors
    4. Operations

    Final Round

  9. The participants must create a commercial for their product along with a company name and logo. Use of templates is not allowed. Any qualifying team found using so shall be disqualified.
  10. Participants will have to pitch their startup company to the investors on a web conferencing platform . They will have 10 minutes to present their complete idea including the commercial, financial projections and marketing plan.
  11. This will be followed by a round of cross questioning on several aspects. The participants should try their best to convince the judges to invest in their company for a reasonable evaluation.
